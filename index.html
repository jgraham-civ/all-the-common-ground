Our structures of education like to divide human knowledge into fields. These fields can be further subdivided; philosophy within the humanities, ethics within philosophy. The same applies to professional disciplines; consider a doctor specialising in surgery, or a mechanic specialising in aircraft. Indeed, our journeys through school, higher education and the workforce are typically ones of increasing specialisation.

This is for good reason—I wouldn’t ask J. Robert Oppenheimer to extract my appendix, nor would I ask a surgeon to design an atomic bomb.

But to assume neither has anything to offer to the other’s field of expertise is counterproductive.

By highlighting all the common ground shared by subject areas in research and current affairs, I hope to show that tackling the world’s most pressing challenges requires an interdisciplinary approach.
